Chart info

Model assumptions, overlays, controls, and interpretation notes.

What this chart shows

This app plots Bitcoin price against time using power-law models. A power law has the form price = A × days^n, where days are counted from Bitcoin genesis on January 3, 2009.

The chart defaults to Giovanni Santostasi's commonly cited model: price = 10^-17 × days^5.82. Other presets are included because public Bitcoin power-law charts use slightly different constants, exponents, and fair-value multipliers.

Why logarithmic scale matters

Bitcoin price has moved across many orders of magnitude. On a logarithmic price axis, equal vertical distances represent equal percentage changes. This keeps early, mid, and projected prices readable on one chart.

Linear scale is available, but it compresses low prices when the chart includes high future projections.

Rainbow bands

The colored zones are multiplier bands around the base power-law curve. They are not separate formulas. They show where price sits relative to the model, from deep-value zones below the base curve to high-risk zones above it.

  • Band prices in the hover popup are calculated from the active model at the cursor date.
  • You can hide bands, edit multipliers, or reset them in settings.
  • Band colors are visual guides, not probability estimates.

ATH / ATL timing overlay

The ATH/ATL overlay is a separate cycle-timing guide. It uses the configured anchor ATL date, then projects an ATH after 1,064 days and the next ATL 364 days later by default.

ATH and ATL prices are derived from model multipliers unless the “Next ATL equals ATH before previous” setting is enabled. In that mode, the following ATL reuses the ATH before the immediately previous ATH.

This overlay is useful for scenario planning, but it is not proof that future cycle timing or prices will match the projection.

Historical data

The cyan line is the loaded BTC daily close data. You can hide it without clearing it, paste newer rows into the CSV box, or use the CoinMarketCap link on the Data tab as a source for missing daily closes.

Controls

  • Mouse wheel over the price axis scales vertically.
  • Mouse wheel over the date axis scales horizontally.
  • Drag the chart to pan.
  • Pinch on mobile to zoom the chart instead of the page.
  • Tap or hover the chart for the price/band popup.
